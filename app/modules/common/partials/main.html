Data Discovery and Processing

You may use the Policy Compass Browser and faceted search to find the appropriate data of what you are interested in.

If the data is not already present in Policy Compass, it would need to be found elsewhere and uploaded to Policy Compass first. You may navigate to Create and then go to Upload Datasets in order to import the data tables into the Policy Compass system and define the indicator that this data represents.

Data Visualisation

Applying datasets to metrics describes a real world situation. The selection of metrics and their construction or the specific selection of data (depending on the starting point) already contains implications on the reasons or the processes behind it. However, you need ways to finding events that could have (had) a causal effect on the real world situation represented by the data. First, searching for possible explanations you could concentrate first on incidents, political decisions, developments in society, etc. that are within the subject area of study. Second, you would look for events that occurred around time points where data shows abnormalities. In Policy Compass, the connection between events and data is made by annotating graph time line with selected events.

Create Causal Models

Assuming you have the necessary data at hand that is related to policies of interest (Evaluate Performance of Policies), you can create a causal policy model using the graphical model editor in the Policy Compass Platform, by adding concepts and their relationships to form a directed graph.

In a causal policy model concepts have underlying datasets related to policies. The relationships can be determined based on your knowledge as well as the data at hand. Another way to develop a causal model is to copy an existing one and modify it. Policy Compass provides a powerful search tool to browse existing user generated content.
